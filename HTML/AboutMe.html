About me

Hi there! I feel pleased you want to learn a little bit more about me. I’m a student at the University of Zurich (Switzerland), where I’m currently enrolled in the master’s program in Computer Science with major Data Science. I have interests in statistics, machine learning & deep learning, computational cognitive modelling, methodology and study design. Making sense of complex data seems fascinating to me and accompanies me in both my academic training and my leisure time!

I started my academic journey at the University of Basel (Switzerland), from where I hold a bachelor’s degree in Psychology. Since the discipline of psychology, contrary to what is often assumed, has little to do with Sigmund Freud and Carl Gustav Jung, but is an empirical science, I was very soon confronted with statistics & research methodology. Working on various studies, I learned how to gather data of high quality and got my hands dirty with statistical software.

In the summer of 2020, I did an internship in Computational Cognitive Modeling at the department of Economic Psychology. I conducted a Parameter Recovery for a cognitive model called Generalized Context Model (GCM; Nosofsky, 1986; the model aims to predict human behavior in categorization tasks). Parameter recovery is the computational modelling equivalent to model evaluation in machine learning. During the internship, I collected around 300 working hours of R coding and could deepen my knowledge in (uni- and multivariate) statistics.

During my bachelor’s I took a lot of extracurricular courses in mathematics (e.g., Calculus, Lin. Alg., Mathematical Statistics, Multivariate Statistics And Machine Learning). I list below some courses I have taken that are relevant to Data Science.

I was privileged to conduct my bachelor's thesis in collaboration with the Max Plank Institute. My bachelor thesis is called "Mathematical Models to Understand Sequential Choice Data derived from the Balloon Analogue Risk Task (BART)" and can be visited here.

After my bachelor's degree, I worked for half a year in the pharmaceutical industry at F. Hoffmann-La Roche AG in Basel. This internship allowed me to combine my passion for cognitive neuroscience and data science. As a member of the Department for Neuroscience and Rare Diseases - Biomarkers and Translational Technologies, I was involved in research on novel biomarkers for dementia.

To get more involved in the field of data analysis, in 2021, I decided to start a master’s in computer science at the University of Zurich (Switzerland). With my prior education in other fields, I was accepted into this master's program under the condition to make up relevant courses from the bachelor's degree in computer science (acceptance under additional requirements). For example, I had to attend courses in data structures and algorithms, software engineering and theoretical computer science. For my major I have chosen Data Science. Thus, I can devote myself to the subject with appropriate mathematical rigor and fidelity to detail.

During my master studies I worked as a working student in the Quant Lab - Systematic Equities at the institutional Asset Manager Bantleon Bank AG / Bantleon AG in Zurich. I worked closely with portfolio managers and the Head of Department, helped automate data operation processes, and created a platform for fund analytics and fund comparison. I worked primarily with R.

During the 2022 semester break, I did a 10 week summer internship at Zürcher Kantonalbank in Zurich. Zürcher Kantonalbank is the fourth largest bank in Switzerland. I was affiliated with the Investment Banking Division in the department “Trading - Equities and Structured Products” and worked closely with the Head of Department. I helped validating the company's option pricing models using empirical data and helped identify potential for gaining market share in the Swiss market for structured products. I worked with SQL and Python (Pandas, Numpy, Dask and more).
